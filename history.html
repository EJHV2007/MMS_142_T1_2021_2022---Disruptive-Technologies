History of AI

The Beginnings of Artificial Intelligence

Artificial intelligence, at its core, aims to imitate human intelligence by way of machines systems. The beginnings of artificial intelligence can be traced back on 1956 in the Darthmouth Summer Research Project in new Hampshire. Researchers from fields of language simulation, neuron nets, complexity theory and more discussed the idea of "thinking machines". The birth of artificial intelligence has affected multiple fields like engineering, mathematics, computer science, and alike.

Artificial Intelligence - A disruptive technology

Disruptive technology is the technology that affects the normal operation of a market or an industry. It displaces a well-established product or technology, creating a new industry or market. In businesses theory, disruptive technology follows the tradition of identifying radical technical change and the creation of tools for its use at a firm or even policy level. As opposed to sustainable technologies, disruptive technologies, affects the way businesses, consumers, and even industries function. Artificial intelligence is a way of simulating the human mind. With electronic capacity and functionality increasing at an exponential rate, the capability of artificial intelligence extends from the simple automation to complex tasks. Its influence has extended to several aspects of human life and which businesses has taken advantage of.

Artificial Intelligence - In industries, businesses, and life

The power of artificial intelligence lies in its speed of execution, power of automation, ability to use complex equations, and accuracy of analysis. In industries, multiple tasks can be done by machines which are controlled by artificial intelligence; increasing production while decreasing cost. In businesses, artificial intelligence is used as predictive models in order to make future decisions. They are also used more intricately when dealing with the consumers of their product by using chatbots or suggesting products based on market trend. In everyday life, artificial intelligence is present in daily activities, such as social media and in simply searching the internet. The platforms you use recommend links or posts based on gathered data and trend.
