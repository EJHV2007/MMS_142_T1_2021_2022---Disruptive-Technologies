How Far We Have Gone

Disruption has always been the next step on the path towards evolution. We, as a society, owe much of our growth to the innovators that have challenged normalcy, severed monotony, and sought methods to improve the way things are done. Creation comes forth from disruption - that is known. Given the leaps they have made in regards to a variety of industries (i.e., medical technology, video games, and education), AI and machine learning are far from their early stages. Researchers from the Dartmouth Summer Research Project in New Hampshire back in 1956, despite their enthusiasm in the idea of "thinking machines," would not have expected any of the innovations made by modern scientists today, such as Richard Sutton on modern reinforcement learning and the researchers under CheXNet. AI is inherently versatile. Research studies further discover methods that allow it to be utilized in situations even outside the realm of science and technology, allowing it and machine learning to grow into a juggernaut of industry, themselves, and a subject of interest to many business sectors. In an article written by Christina Pazzanese for The Harvard Gazette around artificial intelligence, it was reported that worldwide business spending on AI would amass a whopping $50 billion this year and $110 billion annually by 2024 despite the economic crisis occurring in correspondence to the ongoing pandemic. With that in mind, it is safe to say that there is not much to worry about whether its goal to shake things up within and without the science and technology field would persist.
